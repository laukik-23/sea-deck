Young, Digital, Spending

460 million digital consumers who skipped credit cards and went straight to mobile - and 415 million will be middle class by 2030.

Southeast Asia is a full generation younger than Europe or Japan - and unlike those markets, its working-age population is still growing. The Philippines won’t peak until the 2050s. Indonesia until the 2040s. The result is a $263 billion internet economy that tripled its profits to $11 billion in just two years - built on mobile-first infrastructure that looks nothing like the West’s.

Where the World’s Supply Chains Are Moving

Apple, Samsung, and the world’s largest chip makers aren’t considering SEA - they’re already there.

ASEAN captured 17% of global FDI in 2024 - nearly 3x its share a decade ago - while global FDI declined 11%. It has been the #1 FDI destination among developing regions for three consecutive years. The world’s most valuable companies are actively executing China+1 in Southeast Asia, and each country has carved out a distinct position in the global supply chain.

Local Champions, Global Ambition

Local brands beat global giants, and the depth of these markets will surprise you.

In market after market across Southeast Asia, local companies have beaten global giants by adapting faster to local consumers. A Filipino fast-food chain outsells McDonald’s 3-to-1. A Vietnamese coffee brand has 6x more stores than Starbucks. A Malaysian ride-hailing app forced Uber to sell up and leave. A Singaporean e-commerce platform made Amazon irrelevant in the region.

World-class financial infrastructure, distinct investment angles in every economy, and a post-correction entry point that may not last.

ASEAN-6 has the financial infrastructure, the structural growth drivers, and - after an 87% VC correction from the 2021 peak - the valuations. Singapore is the hub through which capital flows into the region, but each economy offers distinct investment angles that most portfolios are not positioned to capture.

The excess has washed out. What remains are rational valuations and quality deals. Post-correction vintages historically outperform. PE deal value surged 60% to $16B in 2024. VCs pivoting from moonshots to 3-4x returns on profitable companies.
Indonesia
Indonesia controls 50% of the world’s nickel - the critical EV battery mineral - and used an export ban to force $30B+ in processing investment onshore, creating an entire domestic industry that didn’t exist a decade ago. Separately, Jakarta is literally sinking, so the government is building a $33B new capital city in Borneo - an infrastructure investment cycle that is just getting started.
Philippines
$38.3B/year flows into the Philippines from 10 million overseas workers - ~8% of GDP. Because this income comes from abroad, domestic downturns barely dent it: during COVID, GDP fell 9.5% but remittances dropped just 0.8%. At post-correction valuations, consumer-facing assets now come with this built-in demand floor priced in.
Thailand
Tourism drives 12% of GDP today and is projected to reach 30%. Thailand isn’t just beaches - Bumrungrad Hospital treats patients from 190+ countries, generating $5-6B/year from medical tourism alone. With post-COVID recovery still accelerating, the healthcare and hospitality sectors are entering their strongest growth phase.
Malaysia
Malaysia created the world’s first halal certification system, now the global standard across 57 nations representing 2 billion Muslims. The halal economy is growing at 6-8% annually to $5T+ by 2030 - and JAKIM certification gives Malaysian companies a regulatory moat into that market that no competitor can replicate.
World-class financial infrastructure through Singapore. Structural investment angles across every economy - from Indonesia’s nickel dominance to the Philippines’ remittance-fuelled consumption. And a post-correction entry point where quality deals are available at rational valuations. The pieces are in place.
